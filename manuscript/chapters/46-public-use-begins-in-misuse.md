## Formal Note XXXVII
### Public Use Begins in Misuse

The instrument will be misused as soon as it becomes available.

This is not a failure of the work. It is the condition of public reality.

A private instrument can remain pure because it is protected by the maker’s intention. A public instrument cannot rely on intention. It will be handled by readers who want speed, by readers who want confirmation, by readers who want novelty, by readers who want proof that the system fails, and by machines that want to complete the pattern before the sentence has finished being received.

The first public fact of the instrument is therefore misuse.

Someone will treat the registry as a list of meanings.

Someone will treat the invariant number as authority.

Someone will classify by the loudest word.

Someone will make Closure the third remainder.

Someone will forget that Closure is also the sixth operator and collapse the axes.

Someone will take a whole paragraph and force it into a single sentence cell.

Someone will ask the model for the answer and accept the fluent receipt without the walk.

Someone will use the fairy tales as examples while forgetting saturation.

Someone will treat the corpus as evidence of certainty rather than training in recognition.

Someone will treat objection as disloyalty.

The instrument must be built to survive all this.

That survival cannot depend on scolding the user. A fragile system calls every misuse external. A stronger system asks what misuse reveals about the places where its public form invites confusion. The work must therefore prepare for misuse not as accident but as diagnostic pressure.

Each misuse shows a boundary.

If the registry becomes a taxonomy, the walk has disappeared.

If the classifier becomes a shortcut, the gate has displaced the route.

If the model becomes an oracle, training has become automation.

If the corpus becomes examples, saturation has become illustration.

If governance becomes filing, authority has lost office.

If objection becomes attack, return has lost tenderness.

The public task is not to prevent all misuse. That is impossible. The task is to make misuse legible enough that repair can begin.

This is why the instrument requires a small set of public refusals.

Do not classify without the sentence.

Do not classify without rereading.

Do not classify without eliminating candidates.

Do not classify a discourse aggregate as if it were one declarative act.

Do not treat the invariant as meaning.

Do not let confidence replace derivation.

Do not let a receipt replace return.

These refusals are not barriers to access. They are the minimum conditions under which access does not become distortion. A reader may enter from anywhere, but the sentence must still be walked. A machine may assist, but the derivation must still answer. A corpus may train, but the example must still remain a sentence, not a decoration for the cell.

The instrument becomes public when these refusals are understandable to people who do not share the archive’s private history.

That is a difficult demand.

The early papers can afford mystery. The late instrument cannot. It may retain provenance pressure, but it cannot require the reader to believe the stable, the field mark, the flash drive, or Arlen’s name in order to use the method. Public use begins where the method no longer depends on the aura of recovery.

A stranger must be able to take a sentence, hold it fixed, walk its contraction, test its landing, and object to the result.

If this cannot happen, the instrument remains authorial.

If it can happen, the instrument has begun to detach from Arlen without betraying him.

This detachment is not abandonment. It is fulfillment. The social-relational child did not want private brilliance. He wanted shareable reality. The whole movement of the archive has been toward a form in which another mind can walk the route without needing to inhabit the original wound.

Public use must therefore be plain.

A sentence is supplied.

The field begins open.

Constraint-bearing units narrow the field.

Closure type is collapsed.

Orientation is collapsed.

Operator is selected by dominance.

The invariant is computed.

The sentence is reread.

Objection may return the derivation to any failed point.

Repair names what survived.

Nothing more is required for first use.

Nothing less is permitted for lawful use.

This is where the instrument’s humility protects it. It does not claim to govern all language. It does not claim to replace grammar, semantics, rhetoric, discourse, therapy, theology, law, or criticism. It governs only a narrow structural question: how a complete sentence terminates. Its strength comes from staying there.

The public temptation will be to expand it too quickly.

Readers will want to classify arguments, lives, stories, institutions, diagnoses, legal cases, conversations, and histories. The archive itself invites this temptation because it shows the same structural pressure at many scales. But invitation is not license. The sentence instrument must remain sentence-level unless a higher-scale instrument is separately built and tested.

This does not forbid analogy.

It forbids smuggling.

One may say that a corpus behaves like a sentence in that it needs governance before closure. One may say that an institution falsely declares finality before inquiry. One may say that a file system repeats the problem of presence and authority. But one may not pretend that these analogies have already been computed by the 54-cell sentence registry.

The public instrument must keep its scope or it will lose its law.

The manuscript should therefore place this warning where the reader can feel it. The work’s images are powerful enough to create overreach. Circle and line. Field and landing. Sentence and corpus. Child and machine. Stable and archive. These can intoxicate. They can make the system feel larger than its earned claims.

Public use begins with sobering.

Use the instrument where it has earned use.

Extend it only where extension has been walked.

Name the difference between analogy and computation.

Name the difference between saturation and proof.

Name the difference between governance and deletion.

Name the difference between receipt and return.

If this discipline is preserved, misuse becomes repairable. If it is lost, the instrument will become exactly what it was designed to resist: a fluent system of false completion.
