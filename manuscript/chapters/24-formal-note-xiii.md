# [Editor’s Note: Second Operator Row Fragment]

The following note opens the second operator family.

Expansion follows Distinction because the two are easily confused. A field may divide and appear to grow. A field may grow by branching. A sentence may separate alternatives or extend reach. The writer therefore treats Expansion only after Distinction has been opened and disturbed.

Expansion is the operation by which a field increases reach, spread, range, distribution, extension, or possible continuation. It is not merely bigness. It is not merely plurality. It is the change by which something goes beyond where it was held before.

## Formal Note XIII
### The Expansion Row Opens

Now Expansion.

Again, only one row.

The Distinction row opened and immediately became more difficult. That is how it should be. Expansion must enter under the same discipline.

Expansion is the operation by which a field increases its reach.

Something spreads.

Something extends.

Something branches outward.

Something multiplies.

Something becomes wider in influence, range, presence, distribution, or possible continuation.

The sentence says:

Do not hold this as confined.

Do not hold it as only here.

Do not hold it as only one path.

Do not hold its effect as local.

Do not hold its future as closed at the first boundary.

Expansion is not the same as Distinction.

Distinction makes difference answerable.

Expansion makes reach answerable.

Sometimes they travel together.

A path splits into two trails.

This distinguishes one path into alternatives. It may also expand the possible routes. The question is primary work.

Now I must hear Expansion through the nine endings.

Expansion-Toward-Is.

Something moves toward increased reach and leaves an expanded condition standing.

The signal reaches farther than before.

The listener inherits a changed condition: the signal now stands as farther-reaching.

Other sentences:

The garden extends to the wall.

The shadow stretches across the floor.

The road continues past the village.

The field opens beyond the gate.

The influence now reaches the committee.

The work is expansion because the held field grows beyond an earlier limit. The orientation is Toward when the sentence gathers to a new extent or boundary. The remainder is Is when what remains is a standing expanded condition.

This cell must be watched for Does.

The signal reached farther than before may be heard as event if the reaching is foregrounded. But if the sentence leaves the signal's range as the main result, Is is stronger.

Expansion-Toward-With.

Something moves toward increased reach and leaves relation.

The crowd moved into the square.

The crowd does not merely grow as a quantity. It enters a place. The listener inherits crowd-with-square, a relation produced by expansion into a field.

Other sentences:

The roots spread into the pipework.

The river widened into a lake.

The conversation opened into a wider question.

The path led into the forest.

The discussion expanded to include the witnesses.

Here expansion reaches a relation. The thing extended becomes held with the field it enters, includes, or opens into.

This cell borders Integration.

The discussion expanded to include the witnesses may be Expansion-Toward-With if the reach of discussion grows. It may be Integration if the witnesses become participants in a structured whole.

Expansion-Toward-Does.

Something moves toward increased reach and leaves occurrence.

The signal reached the far tower.

The sentence leaves the event of arrival beyond the previous range.

Other sentences:

The news reached the coast.

The fire spread to the roof.

The message travelled to the last house.

The crowd filled the hall.

The roots broke through the wall.

These sentences leave expansion as event. Something goes, spreads, reaches, fills, breaks through, or arrives beyond.

The movement is Toward because attention gathers toward the point or result reached. The remainder is Does because the listener inherits occurrence.

Expansion-Across-Is.

Something holds expanded reach across a span and leaves a standing condition.

The road runs across the plain.

The listener inherits the road as extended through a field.

Other sentences:

The network covers the whole district.

The canopy stretches over the courtyard.

The rule extends across all departments.

The stain lies across the fabric.

The river runs through three counties.

Here expansion is not only an event of reaching. It is a condition spread across a span. The remainder is Is.

This cell will often be confused with Relation-Across-Is or Closure-Across-Is when a rule extends across cases. The operation depends on whether reach or settled applicability is foregrounded.

Expansion-Across-With.

Something holds expanded reach across a span and leaves relation.

The railway links towns across the region.

The work is expansion because the line extends regionally, but what remains is towns held with one another through that extended reach.

Other sentences:

The roots connect the trees across the grove.

The message chain links families across the city.

The bridge carries traffic between both banks.

The migration routes connect villages across the valley.

The campaign reached voters across several districts.

This cell is close to Relation because withness is strong. Expansion leads only if the main work is increased reach across a field. Relation leads if the main work is correspondence, fit, balance, or mutual intelligibility.

Expansion-Across-Does.

Something expands through a span and leaves distributed occurrence.

Seeds scattered across the field.

This is the clean case. The action is not one point. It distributes through a field.

Other sentences:

Rumors spread through the school.

Smoke drifted along the corridor.

Light spilled across the floor.

The crowd moved through the streets.

The disease spread across the island.

Here the listener inherits expansion as distributed event or process. The operation increases reach. The orientation is Across because the act holds through a span. The remainder is Does because something happens across that span.

This cell is common and ethically unstable.

Rumors spread.

Disease spread.

Fire spread.

Panic spread.

Expansion may be destructive. The registry must not confuse reach with value.

Expansion-Away-Is.

Something moves away from confinement or prior limit and leaves expanded condition.

The field lies open beyond the fence.

The sentence releases attention away from the boundary and leaves a condition of openness beyond it.

Other sentences:

The road continues beyond the border.

The sound carries past the hill.

The influence extends outside the city.

The roots reach beyond the wall.

The path remains open after the gate.

Here expansion is away from a prior limit. The remainder is Is because what remains is condition: beyondness, openness, extended range.

This cell borders Distinction-Away-Is when the boundary itself is central. Expansion leads when the beyond-reach is central.

Expansion-Away-With.

Something moves away from a prior limit and leaves relation through extension beyond it.

The conversation moved beyond the complaint into a larger question.

The complaint is not simply left behind. It remains related to the larger field that grew from it.

Other sentences:

The local dispute widened into a national argument.

The private grief opened into public memory.

The classroom example expanded into a general rule.

The small error grew into a wider failure.

The first question led beyond itself into another.

This cell leaves withness because the earlier field and the expanded field must be held together. It is Away because the movement departs from the smaller starting point.

This cell is dangerous because expansion may distort origin.

A small error grew into a wider failure may be accurate. It may also exaggerate. The relation between origin and expansion must be inspected.

Expansion-Away-Does.

Something moves away from confinement or origin and leaves an expanding event.

The fire broke out of the shed.

The event is expansion by escape, spread, outbreak, overflow, or release.

Other sentences:

The crowd spilled out of the station.

The river burst its banks.

The sound escaped the room.

The protest spread beyond the campus.

The branches grew out from the trunk.

The listener inherits the act by which something leaves its prior boundary and extends beyond it.

This cell is close to Closure-Away-Does when the movement involves an ending of confinement. But Expansion leads if what matters is increased reach after release.

Now the Expansion row has opened.

Expansion-Toward-Is.

Expansion-Toward-With.

Expansion-Toward-Does.

Expansion-Across-Is.

Expansion-Across-With.

Expansion-Across-Does.

Expansion-Away-Is.

Expansion-Away-With.

Expansion-Away-Does.

The row is vivid because expansion is easy to see.

But vividness is another danger.

The mind may confuse visual spread with sentence-work.

The sentence must still be asked:

What expanded?

From what prior limit?

By what route?

Into what field?

What remains?

Does the sentence leave reach, relation, or event?

Does expansion lead, or does another operator lead?

The row is open.

It must now be disturbed.
