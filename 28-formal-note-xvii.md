# [Editor’s Note: Fourth Operator Row Fragment]

The following note opens the fourth operator family.

Correction is treated after Relation because many corrections work by bringing terms into better relation: dial to reading, claim to evidence, sentence to meaning, act to responsibility. But Correction is not simply Relation. It presupposes a prior standing that is inadequate, misaligned, broken, excessive, incomplete, false, or under test.

Correction is the operation by which a field is changed toward greater answerability.

The writer is careful to preserve the word toward. A sentence may claim correction without actually correcting. The operator names the work attempted or performed in the field; its rightfulness must still be judged.

## Formal Note XVII
### The Correction Row Opens

Now Correction.

This word is dangerous because it sounds virtuous.

To correct seems to improve.

To repair.

To adjust.

To make right.

But a sentence can claim correction falsely.

A person can say:

I was only correcting you.

A system can say:

This adjustment is necessary.

A punishment can call itself correction.

A control can call itself repair.

So Correction must enter carefully.

Correction is the operation by which a prior standing is changed toward greater answerability.

The prior standing matters.

Without a prior standing, there is no correction.

There must be something already placed, claimed, broken, misaligned, mistaken, excessive, vague, unbalanced, or under test.

Correction does not merely change a field.

It changes a field in relation to a standard, pressure, need, error, measure, harm, aim, or adequacy.

The sentence says:

Do not leave this as it stood.

Bring it nearer.

Adjust it.

Repair it.

Revise it.

Test it.

Restore it.

Amend it.

Set it right enough to answer.

Now Correction must pass through the nine endings.

Correction-Toward-Is.

A correction moves toward a new standing condition.

The dial is now aligned.

The sentence leaves a corrected condition standing.

Other sentences:

The record is accurate now.

The picture hangs straight.

The calculation is correct.

The wound is clean.

The route is clear.

In each case, the sentence leaves a prior inadequacy altered into a standing condition. The remainder is Is.

This cell depends on the before-field. Without prior misalignment, inaccuracy, dirt, confusion, or error, the sentence may simply describe a condition rather than correction.

Correction-Toward-With.

A correction moves toward repaired relation.

The key now fits the lock.

The listener inherits a relation brought into adequacy.

Other sentences:

The wheels are aligned with the axle.

The sentence agrees with the evidence.

The schedule matches the train times.

The bandage sits properly against the wound.

The answer corresponds to the question now.

Here Correction leads when the earlier field contained misfit. The remainder is With because what remains is repaired relation.

This cell borders Relation-Toward-With. Relation leads if fit is simply stated. Correction leads if the fit repairs a prior misfit.

Correction-Toward-Does.

A correction moves toward an act of adjustment or repair.

The technician adjusted the dial.

This is the clean case.

Other sentences:

The driver corrected the course.

The editor revised the sentence.

The nurse repositioned the bandage.

The teacher fixed the calculation.

The witness amended the statement.

The listener inherits an event by which a prior standing is changed toward adequacy. The remainder is Does.

This cell is common but dangerous. Adjusted, revised, fixed, corrected, amended: these words can claim repair. The field must decide whether repair is earned.

Correction-Across-Is.

A correction holds across a span and leaves a standing condition.

The claim failed under scrutiny.

The claim is brought through a test and left as failed. The correction is not a single adjustment but a testing across pressure.

Other sentences:

The pattern holds after repeated checks.

The result remains valid under review.

The error appears in every version.

The account fails across all timelines.

The method proves reliable under stress.

Here the sentence changes or confirms a prior standing by carrying it across scrutiny, repetition, review, stress, or comparison. The remainder is Is.

This cell may seem like Closure-Across-Is when a test settles a matter. Correction leads when the standard of testing is central.

Correction-Across-With.

A correction holds across a span and leaves repaired or tested relation.

The revised figures now agree with the records.

The relation between figures and records is brought into answerability across a checking field.

Other sentences:

The translation was checked against the original.

The testimony was compared with the documents.

The route was adjusted to match the terrain.

The schedule was revised around the delays.

The explanation was aligned with the data.

Here Correction moves through comparison and leaves relation. The remainder is With.

This cell borders Relation-Across-With. Correction leads when the relation is produced or tested against prior inadequacy.

Correction-Across-Does.

A correction holds across a span and leaves repeated or distributed correcting action.

The system recalibrates after each reading.

Other sentences:

The teacher corrected errors throughout the essays.

The software flags mistakes as they appear.

The nurse adjusted the dosage over the week.

The editor revised the manuscript line by line.

The machine compensates for vibration during operation.

Here correction is distributed across a process, text, time, or repeated condition. The remainder is Does because the listener inherits repeated corrective action.

This cell may be strong in procedural contexts.

Correction-Away-Is.

A correction moves away from a prior inadequate standing and leaves a new condition.

The claim no longer stands.

The sentence departs from a prior claim and leaves its non-standing as condition.

Other sentences:

The error is gone.

The wound is no longer infected.

The road is no longer blocked.

The old reading is invalid.

The misunderstanding is cleared.

Here the correction is felt as release from prior error, blockage, infection, or misunderstanding. The remainder is Is because what remains is the corrected condition after departure.

This cell borders Closure-Away-Is. Correction leads if the prior inadequacy and its repair are central.

Correction-Away-With.

A correction moves away from a prior standing and leaves transformed relation.

The revised version differs from the draft.

The earlier and later forms are held together through corrective departure.

Other sentences:

The corrected map departs from the old survey.

The new account differs from the first report.

The repaired joint sits differently against the frame.

The amended rule changes its relation to the exception.

The apology changed his relation to the injury.

Here Correction leads if the departure is toward adequacy, repair, or answerability. The remainder is With because before and after, rule and exception, person and injury, remain relationally held.

This cell borders Distinction-Away-With and Relation-Away-With. Correction leads when the difference is corrective.

Correction-Away-Does.

A correction moves away from prior inadequacy and leaves an act of removal, reversal, or repair.

The repair restored the arch.

Other sentences:

The editor removed the error.

The surgeon cleared the blockage.

The driver swerved away from the hazard.

The committee reversed the decision.

The apology repaired the breach.

The listener inherits the event by which the prior field is moved away from error, obstruction, harm, danger, or breach.

This cell is powerful because it includes restoration and reversal. But power makes it ethically charged. A claimed repair may not repair for the person harmed.

Now the Correction row has opened.

Correction-Toward-Is.

Correction-Toward-With.

Correction-Toward-Does.

Correction-Across-Is.

Correction-Across-With.

Correction-Across-Does.

Correction-Away-Is.

Correction-Away-With.

Correction-Away-Does.

The row cannot stand unless the prior inadequacy is visible.

That may be the central test.

What was wrong, incomplete, misaligned, broken, excessive, vague, false, harmful, or under question before the sentence?

What standard governs the change?

Who authorizes that standard?

What remains after correction?

Does the listener inherit repair, or only control wearing repair's name?

The row is open.

It must now be disturbed.
