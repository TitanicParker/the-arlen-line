# [Editor’s Note: First Count of Endings]

This fragment follows the note on direction and remainder. The writer has now separated three questions: what work the sentence performs, which way the sentence moves, and what it leaves behind.

Here the first count appears.

The count is not yet the full registry. The six operators are temporarily set aside so that the writer can inspect only the landing pressure produced by orientation and remainder. This produces nine possible endings. The later lattice depends on this page, but this page is not yet the lattice.

The editor has retained the writer's suspicion of tables. A count is allowed here because it arises from the prior distinctions. It is not yet allowed to become an apparatus of mastery.

## Formal Note IV
### The Nine Ways a Sentence May Come to Rest

The last page ended with three questions.

What work?

Which way?

What remains?

I must now hold the first question aside.

Not abandon it.

Only hold it aside.

The six operators name the work a sentence performs. But before the operators are crossed with anything, I need to understand the landing itself. A sentence may cut, open, connect, repair, gather, or finish. But after any of these acts, it still has to leave the listener somewhere.

So for this page I will ask only:

which way did the sentence move?

what remainder did it leave?

There are three orientations.

Toward.

Across.

Away.

There are three remainders.

Is.

With.

Does.

If each orientation can leave each remainder, then the first count is simple.

Three by three.

Nine.

This is the first count in the sentence work that feels like the geometry notebook.

Not because sentences are triangles.

Not because speech secretly belongs to a drawing.

But because the count protects the field from vague admiration. If I say a sentence can move toward, across, or away, and can leave Is, With, or Does, then I must account for the possible pairings. Otherwise I am only naming impressions.

So I will inspect the nine endings.

Not as a table yet.

As landings.

Toward with Is.

Something moves toward a configuration and leaves a condition standing.

The marker sits on the table.

The sentence gathers attention to a place and leaves the marker there. Nothing further needs to occur for the sentence to have landed. The listener inherits a state.

This is not dramatic.

That is why it is clean.

Something has been placed.

Something now stands.

Toward with With.

Something moves toward a configuration and leaves a relation.

The path splits into two trails.

The sentence gathers attention toward a division, but what remains is not one isolated state. What remains is a relation of alternatives. The listener must now hold one path as becoming two.

The landing is not merely that a path exists.

The landing is that the path is held with its branching.

Toward with Does.

Something moves toward a configuration and leaves occurrence.

The journey reached its end.

Here the movement arrives as event. The sentence does not merely place the journey in a state. It lets the listener inherit the happening of arrival, the completion of travel, the act of reaching.

The remainder is Does.

The sentence leaves something done.

Now Across with Is.

Something holds across a span and leaves a condition standing.

The rule applies every time.

The sentence does not move toward one endpoint. It holds over instances. The listener inherits a standing condition, but that condition is distributed across cases. The Is is not local like the marker on the table. It is rule-like.

It stands by holding across.

Across with With.

Something holds across a span and leaves relation.

The partners adjusted to each other's pace.

The sentence does not move toward one final point. It holds through mutual activity. Each partner is readable through the other. The relation is not merely reached. It is sustained across interaction.

The remainder is With.

The orientation is Across.

This feels central because so much ordinary conversation depends on this kind of landing. People do not only tell one another what stands or what happened. They continually ask one another to hold relations across time, mood, pressure, and response.

Across with Does.

Something holds across a span and leaves occurrence.

The seasons return each year.

The sentence leaves event, but not a single isolated event. It leaves recurrence. The happening belongs to a pattern. The listener inherits a doing that happens across a cycle or distribution.

This is not the same as the journey reached its end.

Both leave Does.

But one moves toward arrival.

The other moves across recurrence.

Same remainder.

Not same orientation.

Now Away with Is.

Something releases from, separates from, opposes, or departs from an earlier condition and leaves a state.

The two sides stand opposed.

The sentence leaves a condition, but the condition is not simple placement. It is separation held as standing. The sides are what they are by being apart, against, or opposed.

The Is remains.

But the movement is Away.

Away with With.

Something releases from a prior condition and leaves relation.

The color faded from blue to gray.

The sentence does not leave only blue, or only gray. It leaves transformation-through. The relation between before and after matters. The listener holds the change as passage from one condition into another.

The remainder is With because the terms are held through transition.

The orientation is Away because the sentence departs from what was.

Away with Does.

Something releases from an earlier condition and leaves occurrence.

The noise died away completely.

The sentence leaves the event of cessation. Sound withdraws. The prior condition ends. The listener inherits not merely silence as a state, but the happening by which the sound ceased.

This is the ninth.

Now I can see the small field.

Toward can leave Is, With, or Does.

Across can leave Is, With, or Does.

Away can leave Is, With, or Does.

Nine endings.

The count returns.

But I must not be too pleased.

A count is not yet proof of completion. It is only a local closure. It says that if these two distinctions are right, then their crossing gives nine forms of landing. It does not yet say that all sentences have been exhausted. It does not yet say that every edge case has submitted. It does not yet give permission to classify too quickly.

The geometry notebook warned me.

A catalogue can close locally and still not be the whole field.

The pair catalogue closed twenty-one joins, but it did not yet know the triple catalogue.

The triple catalogue closed thirty-five selections, but it did not yet spend the right angle.

The right angle locked a waiting length, but it did not yet give the whole field.

Each count had a scale.

This count has a scale too.

It counts the possible crossings of orientation and remainder.

It does not count the operators.

It does not count the full sentence system.

It does not count metaphor, question, command, irony, fragment, silence, or repair.

It only says:

if a sentence moves Toward, Across, or Away,

and if it leaves Is, With, or Does,

then there are nine ways for that movement to come to rest.

That is enough for this page.

I should test whether the nine endings feel different.

The marker sits on the table.

The path splits into two trails.

The journey reached its end.

The rule applies every time.

The partners adjusted to each other's pace.

The seasons return each year.

The two sides stand opposed.

The color faded from blue to gray.

The noise died away completely.

Nine sentences.

Each plain.

Each ordinary.

Each giving a different landing pressure.

If I replace the subjects, the endings should remain.

A cup stands on the shelf.

A committee divides into factions.

A sentence reaches its close.

A law holds across cases.

Two gears mesh under load.

A ritual returns each year.

Two claims stand opposed.

A rumor shifts into accusation.

The fire went out.

Different subjects.

Same landing pressures.

This is the first sign that the endings may be structural.

The subject matter changes.

The landing remains recognizable.

But recognition must be trained carefully.

At first, the examples are too obedient. They were chosen to show the difference. Later the hard cases will matter more.

The hard cases will ask:

Is this really Toward, or does it only look like arrival?

Is this Across, or only repetition in the wording?

Is this Away, or only a negative word?

Does this leave Is, With, or Does?

Does the context change the landing?

Can the same sentence land differently in another field?

These questions must remain open.

A system that cannot be questioned will become another false closure.

I do not want that.

The point of naming endings is not to trap sentences.

The point is to make landings visible enough that another person can inspect them.

If I say:

The sentence moves Away and leaves Does.

Another person should be able to ask:

Where is the departure?

What is the event?

What prior condition has been left?

What remains after it lands?

If I cannot answer, the classification has not landed. It has only been asserted.

That must be the rule.

A name must carry its route.

This returns to the oldest geometry law.

A point may be named O, but the letter is not the point. The letter lets the point be found again.

An ending may be named Toward-Does, but the name is not the landing. The name must let the landing be found again.

Otherwise the name becomes decoration.

The sentence system must not become decorated speech.

It must remain a route another mind may walk.

Now I can see why the nine endings matter.

They are not yet the whole system.

They are the first small landing-field.

They show that a sentence's movement is not exhausted by its operator. The same kind of work can leave different remainders. The same remainder can be reached through different orientations.

A sentence may leave Is by moving toward, across, or away.

A sentence may leave With by moving toward, across, or away.

A sentence may leave Does by moving toward, across, or away.

So sameness has divided again.

Same operator is not same ending.

Same orientation is not same remainder.

Same remainder is not same orientation.

The old law keeps returning because language keeps giving new ways to be falsely satisfied by the word same.

Same in what way?

That question may be the hinge of the whole project.

Now I can write the nine endings plainly, but still without a table.

Toward-Is: arrival into standing condition.

Toward-With: arrival into relation.

Toward-Does: arrival into event.

Across-Is: span that leaves standing condition.

Across-With: span that leaves relation.

Across-Does: span that leaves event or recurrence.

Away-Is: release that leaves standing condition.

Away-With: release that leaves relation or transformation-through.

Away-Does: release that leaves event or cessation.

These phrases are provisional.

They may need to be corrected later.

But they are enough to let the next inspection begin.

If the six operators return, each operator may perhaps move through these nine endings.

I can feel the temptation to multiply.

Six operators.

Nine endings.

But I will not write the number yet.

The multiplication may be true, but a true number can still arrive too early.

The geometry notebooks taught that.

Do not spend tomorrow's proof on today's page.

Today I have only earned nine.

The rest must wait.
