# [Editor’s Note: First Worked Protocol Fragment]

The following note applies the protocol to several ordinary sentences.

The writer does not present these readings as final classifications. They are demonstrations of method. Each reading preserves sentence, field, before, after, operation, orientation, remainder, reason, objection, uncertainty, return, and use. The point is not to prove the registry by example, but to show how the registry slows the reader down.

The fragment is especially valuable because it shows the transition from formal architecture to practical reading.

## Formal Note XXVII
### First Worked Readings Under Protocol

The protocol must now be used.

Not explained again.

Used.

If the registry cannot read ordinary sentences, it is only architecture.

I will begin with simple sentences and let them become less simple.

First reading.

Sentence:

The door is closed.

Minimal field:

Someone is being told the state of a door.

Before-field:

The listener does not know whether the door is open or closed, or the door's state matters.

After-field:

The door is held as closed.

Operation:

Closure, possibly Distinction.

Orientation:

Toward.

Remainder:

Is.

Cell proposal:

Closure-Toward-Is.

Reason:

The sentence brings the door-field to a settled condition. The door is not presented as closing in event, but as closed in state. The listener is asked to hold non-openness as standing.

Objection:

If the sentence merely distinguishes closed from open, Distinction-Toward-Is may compete. If the sentence occurs in a warning, refusal, or privacy context, the exchange-work may differ.

Uncertainty:

Medium without field.

Return:

Yes, for scale.

Use:

Training example.

Comment:

This sentence shows why subject is not enough. Door is not the operation. The same door may distinguish, close, refuse, protect, or warn.

Second reading.

Sentence:

The door closed behind him.

Minimal field:

A person has passed through a doorway; the door then shuts behind him.

Before-field:

The door is open or in motion.

After-field:

The door has shut after the person passes.

Operation:

Closure.

Orientation:

Away.

Remainder:

Does.

Cell proposal:

Closure-Away-Does.

Reason:

The sentence leaves the event of closing as a departure from openness. The behind him adds after-relation: the closure occurs in the wake of movement away.

Objection:

If the field emphasizes separation between him and others, Distinction-Away-With may compete. If the sentence is narrative closure, exchange-work may be larger than the local door event.

Uncertainty:

Low-medium.

Return:

Yes, if narrative context matters.

Use:

Training and scale example.

Comment:

Small changes of wording alter remainder. Is in the first sentence. Does here.

Third reading.

Sentence:

The committee divided into two groups.

Minimal field:

A committee previously held as one becomes held as two groups.

Before-field:

The committee stands as a single deliberating body.

After-field:

Two groups are now held within or after the committee.

Operation:

Distinction.

Orientation:

Toward.

Remainder:

With.

Cell proposal:

Distinction-Toward-With.

Reason:

The sentence distinguishes one body into two groups, but leaves the groups held in relation to the former whole and to one another.

Objection:

If the sentence emphasizes widening activity, Expansion may compete. If the division repairs a procedural problem, Correction may enter. If the division destroys a working body, Integration-Away-Does or Relation-Away-Does may be relevant at another scale.

Uncertainty:

Medium.

Return:

Yes, for context and scale.

Use:

Operator-boundary example.

Comment:

A division does not always belong only to Distinction. The before-field and the consequence matter.

Fourth reading.

Sentence:

The rumor spread through the school.

Minimal field:

A rumor that began with limited reach becomes known or repeated through the school.

Before-field:

The rumor is local, contained, or not yet broadly distributed.

After-field:

The rumor is distributed through the school.

Operation:

Expansion.

Orientation:

Across.

Remainder:

Does.

Cell proposal:

Expansion-Across-Does.

Reason:

The sentence makes reach answerable across a field and leaves the distributed event or process of spreading.

Objection:

The sentence may itself spread the rumor by repeating it. Ethical pressure is high if harm travels with the rumor. If the emphasis is on social damage, further reading is needed.

Uncertainty:

Medium.

Return:

Yes, for harm and evidence.

Use:

Expansion and ethics example.

Comment:

Reporting an expansion may participate in expansion. This must be marked.

Fifth reading.

Sentence:

I know exactly how you feel.

Minimal field:

One person responds to another's expressed or implied feeling.

Before-field:

The listener's feeling has been made present or vulnerable.

After-field:

The speaker claims shared access to that feeling.

Operation:

Relation, possibly Integration or Closure by effect.

Orientation:

Toward.

Remainder:

With.

Cell proposal:

Relation-Toward-With.

Reason:

The sentence offers withness: speaker and listener are placed into a claimed relation of shared feeling.

Objection:

The listener may receive the sentence as erasure. Exactly may overclaim. The sentence may collapse difference, absorb the listener's experience, or close further explanation.

Uncertainty:

High without listener landing.

Return:

Yes, key resistant example.

Use:

Repair and listener-landing example.

Comment:

The speaker's intended relation is not enough. The listener's landing may change the ethical reading.

Sixth reading.

Sentence:

We need to look at the bigger picture.

Minimal field:

A specific concern, detail, injury, or dispute has been raised. The speaker shifts attention to a wider field.

Before-field:

A local matter is open.

After-field:

The listener is asked to hold a wider context.

Operation:

Expansion.

Orientation:

Away or Toward, depending on field.

Remainder:

With or Is, depending on landing.

Cell proposal:

Expansion-Away-With, provisional.

Reason:

The sentence moves away from the local matter toward a wider contextual field, while the local and wider fields remain held together.

Objection:

This may be necessary context. It may also be evasion. It may function as Closure if the local concern is being displaced. It may dilute a specific harm.

Uncertainty:

High.

Return:

Yes, for field and listener landing.

Use:

Scale-shift example.

Comment:

Expansion is not automatically mercy. Wider context may answer the field or avoid it.

Seventh reading.

Sentence:

The apology repaired the breach.

Minimal field:

A relation was damaged; an apology is said to have restored it.

Before-field:

A breach exists.

After-field:

The breach is presented as repaired.

Operation:

Correction.

Orientation:

Away or Toward.

Remainder:

Does or Is, depending on emphasis.

Cell proposal:

Correction-Away-Does, provisional.

Reason:

The sentence presents an act by which the relation moves away from damage toward repair. The apology is the repairing event.

Objection:

Did it repair for the harmed person? Is repair claimed by speaker, observer, institution, or listener? Closure may be smuggled in if the sentence declares the matter finished too soon.

Uncertainty:

High without recipient account.

Return:

Yes, key repair example.

Use:

Ethical repair example.

Comment:

Repair cannot be assigned from repair-language alone.

Eighth reading.

Sentence:

The fragments formed a single account.

Minimal field:

Several fragments are gathered into one account.

Before-field:

The materials are scattered, partial, or separate.

After-field:

They stand as one account.

Operation:

Integration.

Orientation:

Toward.

Remainder:

With or Is.

Cell proposal:

Integration-Toward-With, with possible Integration-Toward-Is.

Reason:

The fragments become organized participation in a whole. With is strong if the parts remain visible in the account. Is is strong if the completed account is foregrounded.

Objection:

The account may erase fragmentary difficulty. The whole may look smoother than the archive permits. Editorial over-integration is possible.

Uncertainty:

Medium-high.

Return:

Yes, editorial self-audit.

Use:

Archive-method example.

Comment:

This sentence reads the present book. It must therefore be handled cautiously.

Ninth reading.

Sentence:

The matter is closed.

Minimal field:

A matter previously open is being declared no longer open.

Before-field:

A question, dispute, process, wound, or responsibility remains active.

After-field:

The matter is presented as closed.

Operation:

Closure.

Orientation:

Toward.

Remainder:

Is.

Cell proposal:

Closure-Toward-Is.

Reason:

The sentence moves the field toward settled non-continuation and leaves closedness as standing condition.

Objection:

Who has authority to close? Was objection heard? Is closure procedural, ethical, emotional, legal, or conversational? The sentence may silence rather than settle.

Uncertainty:

High without route.

Return:

Yes, closure-route example.

Use:

Ethical closure example.

Comment:

This is almost never safe as a bare sentence. It requires route.

Tenth reading.

Sentence:

Someone said today that he got the bus home.

Minimal field:

A reported ordinary utterance about someone returning home by bus.

Before-field:

A small fact of another person's day is not yet shared.

After-field:

The listener holds the reported route home as part of shared reality.

Operation:

Relation, Expansion, or Integration may appear at different scales.

Local sentence-work:

Relation-Toward-Is or Expansion-Toward-Is, provisional.

Field-work:

The sentence brings one person's lived route into another person's shareable field.

Remainder:

Is, with social With beneath it.

Reason:

The content leaves a standing reported condition: he got the bus home. But the act of saying also extends one person's reality into another's and creates a minimal shared field.

Objection:

This sentence may be too ordinary to classify by registry alone. Its importance may be not its local cell but its revelation of speech as shared-reality making.

Uncertainty:

Medium-high.

Return:

Yes, origin example.

Use:

Project-origin example.

Comment:

This is why the registry cannot replace the first perception. The little sentence was not important because it was technically complex. It was important because it revealed that speech makes reality shareable.

Now I stop.

Ten readings are enough for first use.

The protocol did not make the readings faster.

It made them more answerable.

It showed where clean examples become uncertain.

It showed where local reading and exchange-work diverge.

It showed where listener landing matters.

It showed where ethics begins to press through form.

This is the value of the method.

Not certainty at once.

Shareable return.
